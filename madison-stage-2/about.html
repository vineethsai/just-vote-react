About Us

Our Mission Statement

Here at Helping Hands, we want to make the world a better place for everyone to live in. Whether that means protecting the health of the planet, feeding the homeless, or educating our youth, we find all work important. And we want to connect you to that important work. But, finding meaningful volunteer work or charities can be extremely difficult. Our schedules are packed, our resources limited, but our hearts are full. The staff at Helping Hands prides ourselves in connecting people to volunteer opportunities and charities they will find fulfilling and meaningful.

My Story

Volunteering has always played a large role in my life. When I was an infant, my mother took a job at a nonprofit, and I have been helping her with her work for as long as I can remember. In elementary school, my Girl Scout troop would go help out at local soup kitchens. When I entered high school, my teachers placed a heavy focus on volunteer work, and I began to do more unpaid work than paid work. In high school, I volunteered at several organizations, most involving education of some sort, because that's where I found one of my passions. I have found some of my most meaningful volunteer work through outside reccomendations, and that's the service I hope the website will provide. If you live in the Seattle area and enjoy educating, think about some of the following locations, as they happen to have provided me my favorite experiences while volunteering:

Seattle Children's Stanley Stamm Summer Camp: Stamm Camp has started to feel more like home to me than Seattle. For one week, 300 volunteers take 100 children to overnight camp, all of whom have chronic disabilities that prevent them from attending other area camps. During the week of camp, they get to ride horses, catch fish (which they get to take home with them), swim, make arts and crafts, and shoot bows and arrows, all free of charge. More information here.

Team Read: By fourth grade, your reading potential becomes set in stone. If children aren't up to reading level by the end of third grade, they will never catch up with their peers and are therefore more likely to drop out of high school. Team Read's mission is to make sure every child knows how to read before fourth grade. They pair local high school students with one child who needs some extra help, and they work one on one with them once a week for the whole year. Find out more here.

Seattle Parks and Recreation- Evans Pool: While Seattle Parks & Rec has a multitude of different volunteer positions, I found my passion in teaching swim lessons. Work as an assistant instrctor, working side by side with a certified Water Safety Instructor and Lifeguard to teach children of all ages and abilities one of the most important skills they can learn: swimming. Find out more here.
